## Company Overview
NVIDIA Corporation is the leading provider of GPUs and accelerated computing
platforms used in artificial intelligence, data centers, gaming, professional
visualization, networking, and autonomous systems.

The company has become the central infrastructure supplier for the AI boom,
with strong demand for data center GPUs, CUDA software ecosystem advantages,
high margins, and powerful customer relationships with hyperscalers and AI labs.
Its moat comes from hardware performance, software lock-in, developer ecosystem,
and full-stack AI infrastructure capabilities. However, the company also faces
major questions around valuation, cyclicality in semiconductor demand, customer
concentration, competition from custom AI chips, export restrictions, and whether
current AI infrastructure spending can support long-term earnings expectations.

### Howard Marks (Credit/Cycles)
**Verdict:** HARD PASS  |  **Position Size:** Zero; the risk/reward is unattractive at current prices.

The market for NVIDIA exhibits extreme euphoria and low risk aversion, a classic sign that risk is high. The current price reflects immense optimism, offering no margin of safety for a cyclical business, regardless of its quality.

> *"Simply put, risk is low when risk aversion and risk consciousness are high, and high when they’re low."*

### Peter Lynch (Value/Growth)
**Verdict:** PASS  |  **Position Size:** None at current valuation

NVIDIA has a phenomenal growth story as the leader in AI infrastructure. However, the stock price has run far ahead of its earnings, making it too expensive and risky at the current valuation.

> *"When the price line strays far above the earnings line... it means the stock is very expensive, and people are paying an unusually high price for owning Johnson & Johnson."*

### Nick Sleep (Value/Distressed)
**Verdict:** HARD PASS  |  **Position Size:** Zero; fundamental mismatch with investment philosophy.

The company's high-margin, value-capture model conflicts with the 'Scale Economics Shared' philosophy. Furthermore, its high valuation offers no discount to intrinsic value, making it a poor fit despite its strong moat.

> *"Zak and I spend almost no time thinking about stock market indices."*
